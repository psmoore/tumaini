Our Impact

9 focus areas transforming lives in Kagera

Focus Areas

Where We Make a Difference

From education to clean water, each focus area addresses a critical need in Kagera.

Education

25,000 orphans supported

Education is essential for these children to break the cycle of poverty. Although elementary education is free in Tanzania, students need uniforms, paper, books and pencils — items most families cannot afford on $95/year. Tumaini supplies each orphan with school supplies, and provides secondary school fees for the top 10% who qualify.

Clean Water

60+ wells built

24,000 children under five die each year in Tanzania from dirty water. Water sources for most villages are filthy ponds shared by animals and humans. A shallow well at a cost of $5,000 can provide clean water for an entire village. Over the past 14 years, Tumaini supporters have funded over 60 shallow and deep wells.

Sustainable Living

500 people served by Kibehe project

Subsistence farming is how most families survive in Kagera. With climate change and unpredictable rains, crops often die before harvest. Tumaini has funded irrigation projects using solar-powered pumps and above-ground storage tanks, bringing health and economic benefit to entire communities.

Donate to this

Vocational Training

2 training schools

Tumaini operates vocational training schools for tailoring and carpentry, mainly for orphans who leave after primary school. Families have been helped to set up micro-financed businesses such as rearing chickens, goats, cows, beekeeping and fish ponds. Tumaini loans start-up costs at low interest rates.

House Building

$2,800 builds a home

Housing is basic wattle and daub — families share a single room and roofs of leaves collapse in heavy rain. Tumaini builds new homes with foundations of sand and cement, mud brick walls, tin roofs, wood-framed windows with mosquito mesh, and 3 rooms for family privacy.

Transportation

$125 per bicycle

The three-mile walk to and from school can be replaced with a bicycle, saving students an average of two hours per day. Grades improve dramatically. Tumaini has also provided motorcycles for social workers and vehicles for transporting supplies across the 4 offices in Kagera.

Donate to this

Disease Prevention

3,000 children die daily from malaria in Africa

Protection from malaria-carrying mosquitoes is a top priority. Thousands of mosquito nets have been provided. New homes include mosquito protection on windows. Tumaini also works on clean water treatment systems and hygienic sanitation including toilets in schools.

Donate to this

Sewing for Hope

3,500+ dresses sent

The mission of Sewing for Hope is to bring love, joy, and dignity to orphans by providing them with a new, hand-made dress and coordinating t-shirt. These clothes bring great joy and pride — children are eager to venture out in society with dignity. Volunteers sew across the United States.

Future Goals

4,800+ people to be served

Tumaini is partnering with Naples North Rotary Club and Water Mission Tanzania to expand clean water distribution. The new project will quadruple water storage to 40,000 liters and add 7 new taps, with hand washing stations at schools as part of a WASH program.
