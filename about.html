Our Story

About The Tumaini Fund

20 years of hope in Kagera, Tanzania

👑 MBE — Awarded by Prince William, 2013
The Founder

Dr. Susan Wilson, MBE

Founder and CEO of the Tumaini Fund

Dr. Susan Wilson's mission work in Tanzania, one of the ten poorest countries in the world, started in 2001 when she and her family spent four months in Kagera, in the North West Province of Tanzania. She and her husband worked as medical doctors in a local hospital.

The family found themselves shocked at the abject poverty faced by the local Tanzanians. They were even more shocked at the desperate lives faced by AIDS widows and orphans, a result of the widespread violence by the Rwandan, Burundian and Ugandan militia in the 1990s. It was estimated that there were approximately 220,000 orphans at that time.

She has dedicated herself to helping these widows and orphans through the non-profit organizations: Tumaini Fund, UK established in 2003, the Tumaini Fund, USA established in 2009, Tumaini Fund Scotland, and Tumaini Fund Canada.

Dr. Wilson was given the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire (MBE) award by Prince William in 2013 for her outstanding service to alleviate the suffering of so many people in Kagera.

The Crisis

Why Kagera Needs Help

"Tumaini" is the Swahili word for Hope — it is also the name of a charity that seeks to alleviate the terrible suffering of AIDS widows and orphans in Kagera, the most North West province of Tanzania.

The HIV Crisis

HIV/Congo spread from 2-8% to 30% following the attacks on women by Rwandan and Burundian soldiers in the late 1990s. There are now 220,000 orphans in the region. Tanzania is one of the 10 poorest countries in the world.

Subsistence Farming

Ninety percent of Kagera's population are subsistence farmers who survive off their small plots of land with a single crop, expecting a small harvest annually to support 6 to 8 family members. Education is the only way to get out of this cycle.

Water

Water sources for most villages are filthy ponds often used by animals and humans alike. Dysentery and diarrhea are common. Most deaths of children under 1 year of age are due to diarrheal illness from contaminated water.

Housing

Housing is basic wattle and daub built with clay. The roofs, made of grass or banana leaves, cannot withstand the heavy rains. Whole families share a single room, and cooking in the same house creates health and fire hazards.

Where We Work

The African Region of Kagera

Kagera is in the far northwest of Tanzania, bordering Rwanda, Burundi, and Uganda, with Lake Victoria to the east. It is furthest from the source of wealth — far from the tourist trail and the capital Dar Es Salaam.

The Tumaini Fund operates from four offices across Kagera — in Kayanga, Muleba, Chato, and Murgwanza — with 180 social workers serving orphans and widows in surrounding parishes.

Kagera is an area the size of Northern Ireland with a life expectancy of 43 years and a former HIV prevalence of ~30%.
What Guides Us

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

The Tumaini Fund USA's mission is to help the estimated 220,000 impoverished AIDS orphans in the Kagera region of Northwest Tanzania to survive and become self-sufficient by helping to meet their basic needs such as nutritional support, clothing, housing, mosquito nets, access to clean water & educational opportunities.

Our Vision

To develop a healthy, sustainable community in the North-West region of Tanzania. The Tumaini Fund is a Christian Charity which, through faith in Jesus and the power of prayer, raises funds to support the widows and orphans in North-West Tanzania.

How We Help

Our Support Consists Of

1

Adult head-of-family, decent housing, improved farming and nutrition

2

AIDS testing and treatment

3

Income generation — microfinance projects and education to the level each child can achieve

4

Clean water and hygienic sanitation

5

Improving vehicular capability in a remote, mountainous region where most driving is off-road
